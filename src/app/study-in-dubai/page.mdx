<Section title="Intakes in Dubai">

Dubai universities generally follow a flexible, multi-intake academic calendar, which works in favor of Nepali students who may need extra time for visa processing or English test retakes.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">September Intake (Primary Intake)</h3>
This is the main and most popular intake, equivalent to the start of the academic year. Most programs, scholarships, and orientation activities are concentrated around this period.

- Applications open: December – February
- Deadline: May – July (varies by university)
- Classes begin: Late August / September

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">January Intake (Secondary Intake)</h3>
A strong alternative intake offered by most universities, ideal for students who miss the September deadline.

- Applications open: June – August
- Deadline: October – November
- Classes begin: January

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">May/Summer Intake (Limited Availability)</h3>
Offered by select universities, mainly for specific undergraduate and foundation programs, or as a top-up intake for students with all documents ready.

- Applications open: January – March
- Deadline: March – April
- Classes begin: May

We recommend Nepali students apply at least 4–6 months before their preferred intake to allow sufficient time for admission processing, English test scores, document attestation, and visa approval.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">Practical Budgeting Tips for Nepali Families</h3>

Converting NPR to AED and planning a full year of expenses can feel overwhelming, especially with currency fluctuations to account for. A few practical tips can help families plan more accurately:

- Always budget in AED first, then convert. Tuition invoices, accommodation contracts, and visa fees are issued in AED, planning your budget in the local currency first, then converting to NPR for your own reference, avoids confusion when fees are due.
- Account for one-time setup costs. Beyond tuition and monthly living expenses, students should budget separately for one-time costs such as visa fees, medical testing, Emirates ID issuance, security deposits for accommodation, and initial furnishing or setup costs, these can add AED 5,000–10,000 in the first month alone.
- Choose shared accommodation in the first year. Shared apartments or student residences are significantly more affordable than private studios and are a smart way to manage costs while building a social network in a new city.
- Build in a buffer for exchange rate fluctuations. Since fees are charged in AED but most Nepali families earn and save in NPR, it&apos;s wise to add a 5–10% buffer to your budget to absorb currency fluctuations between the time you plan your budget and the time fees are actually due.
- Avoid large, unexplained lump-sum transfers. As outlined in the visa section below, immigration authorities scrutinize sudden large deposits closely, it&apos;s better to build savings gradually and maintain a clear, traceable financial history.

</Section>

<Section title="Dubai Student Visa Guide (2026)">

A UAE Student Visa is mandatory for international students and must be sponsored by either their accredited university or a UAE-resident parent/guardian.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">The Two Main Visa Types</h3>

- **Regular Student Visa:** Valid for 1 year, renewable annually for the duration of the course.
- **Golden Visa:** Valid for 10 years, granted to exceptional graduates and top academic performers (includes family sponsorship benefits).

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">1. Eligibility &amp; Core Requirements</h3>
To apply for visa sponsorship, you must meet the following baseline criteria:

- Unconditional Admission: An official, unconditional offer letter from a UAE Ministry of Education or KHDA-accredited institution.
- Academic &amp; Language Entry: Met all program prerequisites and minimum IELTS/PTE/TOEFL scores.
- Passport Validity: Minimum 6 months validity beyond entry, with at least 2 blank pages.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">2. Required Checklist</h3>
Ensure you have both physical and digital copies of these essential documents organized in one place throughout your studies:

- Valid Passport &amp; passport-sized photographs (UAE specifications)
- Unconditional University Admission Letter
- Attested academic transcripts and certificates
- Official IELTS/PTE/TOEFL score report
- Proof of tuition fee payment or formal fee structure
- Financial Proof: Last 3 to 6 months of bank statements or official sponsor letters
- Completed visa application form (provided by your university)

Critical Financial Warning: Immigration authorities strictly scrutinize bank statements. Avoid sudden, large, last-minute lump-sum deposits with no clear source, as this can trigger immediate delays or visa rejection. Plan your finances well in advance.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">3. Post-Arrival Formalities</h3>
Once you arrive in the UAE on your entry permit, you must complete these steps to stamp your final residence visa:

- Medical Fitness Test: Pass an approved blood test and chest X-ray at an official UAE medical center (required for applicants aged 18 and older).
- Health Insurance: Obtain a UAE-compliant healthcare policy. Most universities offer student insurance packages during enrollment.
- Emirates ID: Apply for your official UAE identity card, which is mandatory for banking, healthcare, and daily residency.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">4. Processing, Renewal &amp; Key Rules</h3>

- Timelines: Processing takes 3 to 5 weeks. Students can choose between Standard and Express processing routes.
- Visa Renewal: Must be initiated from within the UAE at least 45 days before expiry. Requires continued full-time enrollment, active health insurance, and an updated Emirates ID.
- Cancellation &amp; Transfers: If you graduate, withdraw, or face expulsion, your visa is canceled immediately. Overstaying triggers heavy fines. If transferring universities, your old sponsorship file must be closed out entirely before the new institution can apply for a fresh visa.

Note: UAE visa regulations are subject to periodic updates. Always verify current 2026 requirements directly with your university&rsquo;s visa office or ESPOT POKHARA EDUCATION &amp; VISA SERVICES before finalizing travel preparations.

</Section>

<Section title="Work Opportunities While Studying">

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">Current UAE Regulations</h3>
International students are allowed to work on a Study Visa for up to four consecutive hours per day, provided they also obtain a separate Dubai Work Permit. This work permit is not automatic and must be applied for separately, often with employer sponsorship.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">On-Campus Employment</h3>

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">Free Zone Employment</h3>

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">Employer Permissions</h3>
Any part-time or internship work must be formally authorized, informal or undocumented work arrangements are not permitted and can jeopardize a student&apos;s visa status.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">Average Wages</h3>
Part-time wages in Dubai vary by sector but are generally modest, and students should view work opportunities primarily as a way to gain experience and professional contacts rather than a significant source of income.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">Work Restrictions</h3>

Scholarship applications are usually submitted alongside the standard admission application, with some universities requiring a separate scholarship essay or interview for higher-value awards.

- Achieve the highest possible IELTS/PTE score, as some scholarships have tiered English score requirements
- Write a compelling personal statement that highlights achievements, goals, and reasons for choosing the specific program
- Apply to multiple universities to compare scholarship offers before making a final decision
