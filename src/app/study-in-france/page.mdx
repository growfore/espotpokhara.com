<Section title="Why Study in France from Nepal?">

France consistently earns its place among the world's top study destinations, and for Nepali students weighing options in Europe, it deserves serious consideration. Here is why.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">Globally Recognized Degrees with World-Class Universities</h3>

A degree from France grants you instant international credibility, powered by elite institutions like PSL University Paris (#28 globally) and Sorbonne University (#72). Nepali students can choose between broad-based, highly affordable public universities and prestigious, selective Grandes Écoles known for elite business and engineering networks.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">Affordable Tuition Fees at Public Universities</h3>

Because the French government heavily subsidizes public higher education, international students pay only a fraction of typical study costs. Annual tuition at public universities ranges from just €170 to €3,770 depending on your degree level, providing unbeatable global value compared to the UK or US.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">Over 1,500 English-Taught Programs</h3>

You do not need to be fluent in French to access world-class education, as France offers more than 1,500 degree tracks taught entirely in English. However, learning basic French is highly recommended to strengthen your visa file, ease daily life, and open up significantly more local job opportunities.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">Part-Time Work Rights While Studying</h3>

International students are legally permitted to work up to 964 hours per year, which averages out to 20 hours per week during semesters and full-time during breaks. With the French minimum wage (SMIC) set at €12.31/hour gross, you can easily earn €600–€900 monthly to cover your living expenses.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">Post-Study Work Pathway (APS / RÉCE)</h3>

After completing a French master's degree, graduates from Nepal can transition to the 12-month RÉCE post-study permit. This visa grants you full-time working rights to hunt for a job, launch a business, or start a professional career anywhere in France without immediate visa pressure.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">Pathway to Permanent Residency in France</h3>

Building a long-term life in France is highly structured: after 5 years of continuous legal residence and working on a standard residency permit or Passeport Talent, graduates become eligible to apply for a 10-year permanent residence card (Carte de Résident) or full French citizenship.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">Scholarships - Including Fully Funded Options</h3>

Multiple funding opportunities target high-achieving international applicants, including the elite France Excellence Eiffel Scholarship, which provides up to €1,200/month for master's tracks and €2,100/month for PhDs. Erasmus+ and Campus France also provide substantial tuition waivers and living stipends.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">Gateway to All of Europe</h3>

Holding a French student visa gives you unrestricted access to the entire 27-country Schengen Area. Studying at the geographic heart of Europe allows you to freely travel, network, and pursue competitive internship or career opportunities across the entire continent.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">Vibrant, Safe, Multicultural Environment</h3>

France hosts a diverse global student community with active, welcoming Nepali student networks well-established in hubs like Paris and Lyon. Outside the classroom, students enjoy an exceptionally high quality of life, celebrated for its safe urban spaces, world-famous cuisine, and rich cultural heritage.

<Section title="Intakes in France for Nepali Students 2026–2027">

France operates primarily one main academic intake per year, unlike some destinations that have multiple windows.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">September / October Intake, Primary Intake</h3>

The autumn window is France's main intake, offering the largest selection of bachelor's and master's programs, university seats, and financial aid. Regular applications run from January to June, but major scholarship deadlines (like the Eiffel Scholarship) close much earlier, between October and January of the preceding year.

Timeline for September 2027 Intake:
- Sept–Oct 2026: Begin language test preparation (IELTS or French) and research target universities.
- Oct–Dec 2026: Prepare admission documents and apply for early scholarship windows.
- Jan–Apr 2027: Submit standard university applications and organize your financial proofs.
- May–June 2027: Receive your official admission letters and apply for your student visa in Kathmandu.
- September 2027: Arrive in France and begin your classes.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">January / February Intake, Secondary Intake</h3>

A limited number of programs, primarily at private institutions, business schools, and some master's programs, offer a January or February intake. This is not universal and must be confirmed for each specific program. Application deadlines for this intake typically fall September–November.

France offers top-tier programs in AI, Data Science, and Cybersecurity through specialized institutions like IP Paris and Grenoble INP. This thriving tech sector provides international graduates with abundant internship pathways and rapid routes to full-time employment.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">Culinary Arts & Hospitality Management</h3>

As the world's culinary capital, France offers unmatched global prestige for students studying Gastronomy, Pastry Arts, or Hotel Management. Elite institutions like Le Cordon Bleu and Ferrandi Paris combine intense technical training with premium industry placements. A qualification from these schools opens direct career pathways into global luxury hospitality and Michelin-starred establishments.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">Fashion Design & Luxury Brand Management</h3>

Paris stands as the undisputed center of global fashion, making it the premier destination for studying Fashion Design and Luxury Brand Management. <h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">Political Science, International Relations & Law</h3>

Sciences Po Paris ranks among the top five institutions globally for politics, serving as a premier training ground for diplomacy, public policy, and international law. The university offers highly diverse, English-medium tracks that actively recruit talent from across Asia. A degree here carries immense structural weight with international NGOs, governments, and global legal bodies.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">Natural Sciences, Medicine & Health</h3>

Led by historic institutions like Sorbonne University and Paris Cité, France is a global powerhouse for biomedical research, physics, and chemistry. While core medical degrees remain highly competitive and require fluent French, fields like Public Health and Biomedical Engineering offer accessible English-taught master's tracks. Students enjoy access to heavily funded, state-of-the-art laboratory ecosystems.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">Architecture & Urban Planning</h3>

Programs seamlessly blend historical preservation with cutting-edge urban engineering. Furthermore, a French architecture degree is fully recognized across the European Union, granting graduates complete professional mobility.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">Social Sciences, Humanities & Arts</h3>

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">Financial Proof Required for France Student Visa</h3>

The French government requires Nepali students to demonstrate they can financially support themselves in France. The minimum financial requirement is €615/month (~€7,380/year) for living costs, plus full tuition fees for your program. You must show this as bank balance (personal or sponsor's), scholarship letter, or a combination.

Unlike Germany, France does NOT require a separate blocked account. Your regular bank statements, sponsor's bank statements, or scholarship letter serve as financial proof, provided they clearly demonstrate the required funds. A higher balance than the minimum strengthens your application significantly.

<Section title="France Student Visa - Complete Process & Requirements for Nepali Students 2026–2027">

To study in France, Nepali students require a Long-Stay Student Visa (Visa de Long Séjour, VLS-TS pour études). This visa functions as a temporary residence permit for the duration of your studies and must be renewed annually or converted to a Carte de Séjour upon arrival.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">Types of French Student Visa</h3>

Visa de Long Séjour Temporaire pour Études (VLS-T): For programs of more than 3 months and up to 6 months. No separate residence permit needed.

Visa de Long Séjour valant Titre de Séjour pour Études (VLS-TS): For programs of more than 6 months. This is the standard student visa for Nepali students pursuing bachelor's, master's, or doctoral degrees. It serves as a residence permit for the first year and must be validated online within 3 months of arrival in France via ANEF (Administration Numérique pour les Étrangers en France). It is then renewable annually.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">The Campus France Process, Mandatory for Nepali Students</h3>

Unlike Germany or Australia, France requires international students to go through Campus France before applying for a student visa. Campus France is the official French agency for international student mobility. Campus France Nepal operates and supports Nepali students through this process.

Step 1: Create a Campus France account at the dedicated Campus France Nepal portal.

Step 2: Enter your academic background, language proficiency, and program choices.

Step 3: Attend a Campus France interview in Kathmandu. The Campus France counselor will assess your study plan, language preparation, and academic profile.

Step 4: Receive your Campus France validation (CEF - Confirmation d'Études en France). This confirmation is required before you can apply for the visa at the French Embassy or VFS.

The Campus France process typically takes 4–8 weeks including the interview wait time. Start this process as soon as you receive your university admission letter, or even before, as Campus France can be initiated before final admission confirmation at some stages.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">Academic Requirements</h3>

For Bachelor's (Licence) programs:
- Completed +2 / Higher Secondary Certificate (NEB or equivalent)
- Minimum GPA: 2.8–3.0 (approximately 70% or 7.0 CGPA out of 10)
- Gap year: Most French universities allow up to 2 years of gap for bachelor's applicants
- Some universities may require entrance exams

For Master's programs:
- Completed bachelor's degree in a relevant field
- Minimum 70% or above in bachelor's degree (varies by institution and program)
- Gap year: Most universities allow up to 3 years for master's applicants
- Letters of recommendation, Statement of Purpose, and portfolio (for arts/design programs)

For Doctoral programs:
- Completed master's degree or equivalent
- Research proposal
- Supervision agreement from a French academic supervisor

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">Language Requirements</h3>

For French-taught programs:
- DELF B2 or DALF C1: Most widely accepted for bachelor's and master's in French
- TCF (Test de Connaissance du Français) or TEF: Accepted at some institutions
- French proficiency at minimum B2 level; C1 recommended for competitive programs

For English-taught programs:
- IELTS Academic: minimum 6.0–6.5 (7.0+ for top institutions like HEC Paris, Sciences Po)
- TOEFL iBT: minimum 80–90
- PTE Academic: 58–65
- Some institutions accept MOI (Medium of Instruction) letters in lieu of standardized test, check individual university requirements

Important note: Even if your program is English-taught, learning French to A1–B1 level is strongly recommended. It dramatically improves your daily life in France, opens more internship and job opportunities, and strengthens your Campus France interview.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">Financial Proof Requirements for France Student Visa</h3>

The French Embassy requires proof that you can financially support yourself during your studies. Minimum required: €615/month (~€7,380/year) for living costs, plus your full tuition fees. Documents accepted:

- Personal bank statements (last 6 months) showing sufficient balance
- Sponsor's bank statements (parent or guardian), most common for Nepali students
- Scholarship letter from a recognized French or international scholarship body (e.g., Eiffel, Erasmus+)
- Letter from employer (if self-supporting)
- Property ownership or land documents as supporting evidence of financial stability

All Nepali-language documents must be accompanied by certified English translations. Translations must be done by a certified translator and notarized.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">Complete Visa Document Checklist for Nepali Students</h3>

The following documents are required for the French Long-Stay Student Visa application. Submit two complete organized sets unless otherwise specified.

Mandatory documents:

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">How to Apply for the France Student Visa from Nepal</h3>

Step 1: Create your Campus France account and attend the Campus France interview in Kathmandu. Receive your CEF.

Step 2: Complete your visa application online at france-visas.gouv.fr. Select Long-Stay Visa (VLS-TS), Student.

Step 3: Book your visa appointment at the French Embassy or VFS Global in Kathmandu. French Embassy address: Lazimpat, Kathmandu.

Step 4: Attend your appointment. Bring all documents in two organized sets. Submit biometric data. Pay the visa fee (approximately €99, paid in NPR).

Step 5: Wait for the visa decision. Standard processing: 4–8 weeks.

Step 6: Upon receiving your passport with visa, validate your VLS-TS online within 3 months of arrival at france-visas.gouv.fr/en_US/page/validate-your-visa.html, failure to validate makes your visa invalid for residence purposes.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">How Long Does the France Student Visa Take?</h3>

Standard processing time: 4–8 weeks from complete application submission. Allow extra time during peak months (July–September) when volumes are highest. Begin the Campus France process immediately after receiving your admission letter.

<Section title="Post-Study Work Visa for Nepali Graduates in France (APS / RÉCE) 2026–2027">

One of France's strongest selling points for international students is the structured post-study pathway, and for Nepali master's graduates specifically, this is a genuine route into European employment and long-term settlement.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">1. What is the RÉCE (formerly APS)?</h3>

A 12-month, non-renewable temporary residence permit allowing non-EU graduates to stay in France to find a job or start a business.

- Work Rights: Full-time work (up to 964 hours/year) in any field.
- Entrepreneurship: Register a company via URSSAF.
- Travel: Free movement within the Schengen Area.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">2. Rules for Nepali Students</h3>

- Degree Needed: Master's, Grande École engineering diploma, or a Licence Professionnelle (+12 months student work history).
- Important Twist: Unlike Indian graduates who get up to 24 months via bilateral pacts, Nepali graduates fall under the standard 12-month framework. Double-check details with Campus France Nepal.
- When to Apply: Online via the ANEF portal (anef.interieur.gouv.fr) 2 months before your student visa expires. You will receive an immediate Attestation de Dépôt to keep your stay legal.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">3. Switching to a Long-Term Work Permit</h3>

Once you secure a contract, you must transition to one of these three primary tracks:

- Carte de Séjour Salarié (Standard Work Permit): Requires a gross salary of at least €2,800/month (1.5x SMIC). Your job must match your studies to bypass local labor market blocks. If your salary falls below this mark, your employer is required to pass a mandatory 3-week local recruitment test before sponsoring you.
- Passeport Talent (Skilled Employee / Graduate Track): Requires an annual gross salary of at least €39,582. This option is valid for up to 4 years and completely bypasses the standard local labor market test. A major perk: your spouse receives automatic, full working rights.
- EU Blue Card (High-Skilled Professional): Requires a minimum contract of 6 months and a gross annual salary of at least €59,373. This premium route completely waives the labor market test and unlocks the fastest track toward long-term mobility and working rights across other EU member states.

</Section>

<Section title="Pathway to Permanent Residency in France">

After 5 years of continuous legal residence in France (combining student residence + work residence), graduates can apply for the Carte de Résident, a 10-year permanent residency permit. Requirements include:

- 5 years of continuous legal residence
- Stable income and employment
- Basic French language proficiency
- Demonstrated integration into French society
- No serious criminal record

French citizenship is possible from 5 years of residence with language proficiency (B1–B2 French), civic knowledge, and integration. France offers one of the most accessible routes to European citizenship for qualified international graduates.

</Section>

<Section title="Permanent Residency (PR) in France for Nepali Students">

France offers a structured and achievable PR pathway for international graduates who build careers in the country. Here is the complete roadmap.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">Phase 1, Study in France (2–5 years)</h3>

Complete your bachelor's (3 years) or master's (1.5–2 years) degree at a recognized French institution. During your studies, develop your French language skills (aim for B2 or higher), complete your mandatory internships, build a professional network, and get Werkstudent/part-time experience. These directly accelerate your post-graduation outcomes.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">Phase 2, APS/RÉCE Post-Study Permit (12 months)</h3>

Immediately after graduation, apply for the APS/RÉCE permit before your student residence expires. Work actively, part-time, internships, or full-time, while job searching. Most master's graduates in business, engineering, IT, and health find qualifying employment within the 12-month window.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">Phase 3, Transition to Work Permit</h3>

Secure a qualifying job offer and transition to a Salarié work permit (1.5x SMIC minimum salary) or Passeport Talent (2x SMIC - recommended for faster PR path and family rights).

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">Phase 4, Permanent Residency</h3>

After 5 years of continuous legal residence in France (combining student + work residence), apply for the Carte de Résident (10-year permanent residency). Requirements: stable income, French language proficiency (B1 minimum), civic integration, clean criminal record.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">Phase 5, French Citizenship</h3>

After 5 years of legal residence with B1–B2 French proficiency and demonstrated civic integration. French citizenship is one of Europe's most valued, it confers the right to live and work in all 27 EU member states.

For Nepali students in engineering, IT, business, health sciences, and aerospace who combine strategic degree choice, strong French language skills, and proactive career building during their studies, the pathway from Nepal to French permanent residency is realistic and achievable within 7–10 years.

</Section>
