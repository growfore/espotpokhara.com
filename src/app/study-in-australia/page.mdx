<Section title="Intakes in Australia for Nepali Students">

Australian universities offer multiple intakes throughout the year, giving Nepali students flexibility in choosing when to begin their studies.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">February Intake (Primary Intake)</h3>

The February intake, also known as Semester 1, is the main intake for most Australian universities. It runs from late February to June/July. The majority of degree programs, courses, and scholarships are available during this intake. Applications typically open from July to November of the preceding year. This is the recommended intake for Nepali students applying to undergraduate and postgraduate degrees.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">July Intake (Secondary Intake)</h3>

The July intake, Semester 2, runs from July to November/December. While course availability is slightly reduced compared to February, many universities offer popular programs during this intake. It is ideal for students who missed the February deadline or who need extra preparation time. Applications typically open from January to April.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">November Intake (Limited Options)</h3>

Some universities and TAFE (Technical and Further Education) institutions in Australia offer a November or Summer intake. This is primarily for short courses, bridging programs, and select vocational qualifications. It is not available at all universities and the course range is limited. Espot Pokhara Education & Visa Services can help you identify which institutions offer a November intake for your preferred course.

<Section title="Australia Student Visa (Subclass 500), Process & Requirements for Nepali Students">

All international students studying in Australia for more than three months must hold a Student Visa (Subclass 500). Here is everything Nepali students need to know about the Australia student visa process.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">Academic Requirements</h3>

To be eligible for the Subclass 500 visa, you must hold a Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE) from an Australian institution registered with the Commonwealth Register of Institutions and Courses for Overseas Students (CRICOS). Academic entry requirements vary by university and course level:

- Undergraduate programs generally require completion of +2 (Grade 12) or equivalent with minimum required grades.
- Postgraduate programs generally require a relevant bachelor's degree with minimum GPA requirements.
- Some universities also require specific subject prerequisites for certain courses.

- Your ties to Nepal indicate your intention to comply with visa conditions

<Section title="Permanent Residency (PR) in Australia for Nepali Students">

Permanent Residency in Australia is one of the most sought-after goals for Nepali students who choose to study there. Australia's skilled migration system rewards international graduates with Australian qualifications and work experience. Here is an overview of the main PR pathways.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">Common PR Pathways After Graduating in Australia</h3>

<h4 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-4 mb-2">1. Skilled Independent Visa (Subclass 189)</h4>

A points-tested visa for skilled workers not sponsored by an employer or family member. Requires a minimum of 65 points on the SkillSelect points test. Points are awarded for age (under 45), English proficiency, work experience, Australian study, and nominated occupation. Many Nepali graduates in IT, engineering, accounting, and nursing qualify for this visa.

<h4 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-4 mb-2">2. Skilled Nominated Visa (Subclass 190)</h4>

Requires nomination by an Australian state or territory government. In exchange for a state nomination, applicants receive 5 additional points. Different states have different occupation in-demand lists, Espot Pokhara helps students identify which state best suits their occupation and qualifications.

<h4 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-4 mb-2">3. Employer-Sponsored Visa (Subclass 482 / 186)</h4>

Students who find a sponsoring employer in Australia may apply for the Temporary Skill Shortage Visa (Subclass 482) or the Employer Nomination Scheme (Subclass 186) for direct PR. Finding a sponsoring employer is easier in shortage occupations such as nursing, engineering, and IT.

<h4 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-4 mb-2">4. Graduate Temporary Visa (Subclass 485) as a Bridge</h4>

The Subclass 485 visa provides time to accumulate Australian work experience and improve SkillSelect points before applying for PR. Most Nepali graduates use this visa strategically to strengthen their skilled migration application.

Key Point: Your choice of course, institution, city, and occupation directly affects your PR eligibility and timeline. Espot Pokhara provides personalized PR pathway planning from Day 1 of your counselling session.
