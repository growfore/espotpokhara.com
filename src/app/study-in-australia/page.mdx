## Intakes in Australia for Nepali Students

Australian universities offer multiple intakes throughout the year, giving Nepali students flexibility in choosing when to begin their studies.

### February Intake (Primary Intake)

The February intake, also known as Semester 1, is the main intake for most Australian universities. It runs from late February to June/July. The majority of degree programs, courses, and scholarships are available during this intake. Applications typically open from July to November of the preceding year. This is the recommended intake for Nepali students applying to undergraduate and postgraduate degrees.

### July Intake (Secondary Intake)

The July intake, Semester 2, runs from July to November/December. While course availability is slightly reduced compared to February, many universities offer popular programs during this intake. It is ideal for students who missed the February deadline or who need extra preparation time. Applications typically open from January to April.

### November Intake (Limited Options)

Some universities and TAFE (Technical and Further Education) institutions in Australia offer a November or Summer intake. This is primarily for short courses, bridging programs, and select vocational qualifications. It is not available at all universities and the course range is limited. Esport Consultancy can help you identify which institutions offer a November intake for your preferred course.

## Australia Student Visa (Subclass 500) — Process & Requirements for Nepali Students

All international students studying in Australia for more than three months must hold a Student Visa (Subclass 500). Here is everything Nepali students need to know about the Australia student visa process.

### Academic Requirements

To be eligible for the Subclass 500 visa, you must hold a Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE) from an Australian institution registered with the Commonwealth Register of Institutions and Courses for Overseas Students (CRICOS). Academic entry requirements vary by university and course level:

- Undergraduate programs generally require completion of +2 (Grade 12) or equivalent with minimum required grades.
    
- Postgraduate programs generally require a relevant bachelor's degree with minimum GPA requirements.
    
- Some universities also require specific subject prerequisites for certain courses.
    

### English Language Requirements (IELTS, PTE, Duolingo, TOEFL)

Proof of English language proficiency is mandatory for the Australian student visa. Most universities accept the following:

|English Test|Minimum Required Score (Typical Australia Requirements)|
|---|---|
|**IELTS Academic**|Overall 6.0 – 6.5, with no band below 5.5 – 6.0|
|**PTE Academic**|Overall 50 – 58|
|**TOEFL iBT**|Overall 64 – 79|
|**Duolingo English Test (DET)**|95 – 110|

Exact score requirements depend on the institution and course. Nursing and other health-related courses typically require higher English scores.

### Financial Proof Requirements (Bank Statement, AUD 29,710)

You must show you have sufficient funds to cover tuition, living expenses, and return travel. The Australian government currently requires evidence of at least AUD 29,710 for living costs per year (for the primary applicant), in addition to your tuition fees. Acceptable evidence includes:

- Bank statements (personal or family savings)
    
- Education loan approval letters
    
- Scholarship letters
    
- Financial guarantee from sponsor (with supporting documents)
    

Funds must typically be demonstrated as available and accessible, not encumbered or newly deposited.

### Genuine Student (GS) Requirement

Since November 2023, Australia has replaced the Genuine Temporary Entrant (GTE) requirement with the Genuine Student (GS) requirement. This is a statement you submit with your visa application explaining:

- Why you have chosen your particular course and institution
    
- How the course relates to your career goals
    
- Your ties to Nepal indicate your intention to comply with visa conditions
    
- Your understanding of your visa obligations
    

The Genuine Student statement is a critical part of your visa application. Esport Consultancy provides expert guidance and review of your GS statement to maximize your visa approval chances.

### Health Insurance - OSHC (Overseas Student Health Cover)

All international students in Australia must hold Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) for the full duration of their student visa. OSHC covers basic medical services, hospital care, and some prescription medications. Approved providers include Medibank, Bupa, CBHS, Allianz Care, and nib. Cost is approximately AUD 600–840/year for a single student. Your university may arrange OSHC automatically, confirm with your institution.

## Permanent Residency (PR) in Australia for Nepali Students

Permanent Residency in Australia is one of the most sought-after goals for Nepali students who choose to study there. Australia's skilled migration system rewards international graduates with Australian qualifications and work experience. Here is an overview of the main PR pathways.

### Common PR Pathways After Graduating in Australia

1. **Skilled Independent Visa (Subclass 189):** A points-tested visa for skilled workers not sponsored by an employer or family member. Requires a minimum of 65 points on the SkillSelect points test. Points are awarded for age (under 45), English proficiency, work experience, Australian study, and nominated occupation. Many Nepali graduates in IT, engineering, accounting, and nursing qualify for this visa.
    
2. **Skilled Nominated Visa (Subclass 190):** Requires nomination by an Australian state or territory government. In exchange for a state nomination, applicants receive 5 additional points. Different states have different occupation in-demand lists — Esport Consultancy helps students identify which state best suits their occupation and qualifications.
    
3. **Employer-Sponsored Visa (Subclass 482 / 186):** Students who find a sponsoring employer in Australia may apply for the Temporary Skill Shortage Visa (Subclass 482) or the Employer Nomination Scheme (Subclass 186) for direct PR. Finding a sponsoring employer is easier in shortage occupations such as nursing, engineering, and IT.
    
4. **Graduate Temporary Visa (Subclass 485) as a Bridge:** The Subclass 485 visa provides time to accumulate Australian work experience and improve SkillSelect points before applying for PR. Most Nepali graduates use this visa strategically to strengthen their skilled migration application.
    

> **Key Point:** Your choice of course, institution, city, and occupation directly affects your PR eligibility and timeline. Esport Consultancy provides personalized PR pathway planning from Day 1 of your counselling session.

### What IELTS score is required for the Australia student visa?

Most Australian universities require a minimum IELTS Academic overall band score of 6.0–6.5, with no individual band below 5.5–6.0. Some competitive programs (such as nursing, law, or top-ranked universities) may require 6.5 or 7.0. For the student visa itself, the Department of Home Affairs does not set a specific IELTS score — rather, you must meet your institution's English requirement and receive a CoE. ### Can Nepali students work while studying in Australia?

Yes. International students on a valid Subclass 500 visa can work up to 48 hours per fortnight during the academic semester and unlimited hours during scheduled academic breaks. The Australian minimum wage currently stands at approximately AUD 23.23/hour. Many Nepali students work in hospitality, retail, aged care, and delivery roles to supplement their income.

### How do I apply for a student visa for Australia from Nepal?

To apply for the Australia Student Visa (Subclass 500) from Nepal, you must:

1. Receive a Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE) from a CRICOS-registered institution.
    
2. Create an ImmiAccount on the Department of Home Affairs website.
    
3. Complete the online visa application and upload all required documents including your GS statement, English test results, financial evidence, and OSHC.
    
4. Pay the visa application fee.

### What is the Genuine Student (GS) requirement for Australia?

The Genuine Student (GS) requirement replaced the Genuine Temporary Entrant (GTE) requirement in November 2023. As part of your Subclass 500 visa application, you must submit a written statement explaining why you have chosen your specific course and institution, how it relates to your career plans, and why you are applying for a student visa. The statement should demonstrate that your primary intention is to study. A poorly written GS statement is one of the most common reasons for student visa refusal.
