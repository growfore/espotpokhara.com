<Section title="Language Class Requirements for International Students">

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">Japanese Language Proficiency Test (JLPT)</h3>

The Japanese-Language Proficiency Test, or JLPT, is an internationally known standardized test to evaluate and confirm Japanese language proficiency for non-native speakers. The JLPT in Japan is managed and supervised by the Ministry of Education through the Japan Educational Exchanges and Services (JEES). The Japan Foundation tests administrate with local cultural exchange and educational institutions. Anyone can sit for the JLPT as there is no age limit for this test.

<h4 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-4 mb-2">N1: Advanced Proficiency</h4>
The N1 level of the Japanese Language Proficiency Test (JLPT) is the highest and most advanced level academically and professionally, showcasing Japanese language skills. At the N1 level, students equip themselves with advanced reading and listening skills, including the ability to understand complex literature and academic materials. The students are skilled in using difficult grammar, know many words, and are experts at reading and writing Japanese characters (kanji). Passing the N1 test shows you are excellent at Japanese and can do well in school and important jobs where you need to speak Japanese. People will know you are excellent at the language, which opens up more work and learning opportunities for you in Japan.

<h4 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-4 mb-2">N2: Upper Intermediate Proficiency</h4>
The N2 level of the Japanese Language Proficiency Test (JLPT) is a significant milestone in Japanese proficiency, indicating upper-intermediate skills. To pass this test, a solid foundation of everyday grammar and vocabulary is necessary. Individuals at this level should be able to understand regular content read and heard in Japanese. Achieving the N2 level is valuable for those interested in studying or working in Japan, as it signifies a commendable proficiency in the language. N2 proficiency brings forth enhanced educational and career opportunities by unlocking doors to various Japanese-speaking environments.

<h4 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-4 mb-2">N3: Lower Intermediate Proficiency</h4>
The N3 level of the Japanese Language Proficiency Test (JLPT) signifies lower-intermediate language proficiency. To pass it, students should grasp basic grammar and everyday words, demonstrating an understanding of simple, everyday situations in Japanese. The N3 level assesses basic grammar, vocabulary, reading comprehension, and listening skills essential for daily tasks and regular communication. Achieving proficiency at this level ensures the ability to use the language effectively in daily life.

<h4 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-4 mb-2">N4: Basic Skills in Japanese</h4>
The N4 level of the Japanese Language Proficiency Test (JLPT) indicates basic skills in Japanese. To pass this test, students should know simple grammar and vocabulary, possess proper listening skills, and understand the words they use in daily life. Achieving proficiency at the N4 level demonstrates a foundational understanding of Japanese, enabling individuals to navigate basic communication and comprehend everyday expressions. This level serves as a crucial starting point for language learners on their journey to mastering Japanese.

<h4 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-4 mb-2">N5: Elementary Proficiency</h4>
N5 represents the most basic level of Japanese proficiency. To pass it, you should know elementary grammar and basic words and be able to understand simple things you read and hear. The N5 level serves as a starting point for learning Japanese, facilitating very basic communication and understanding simple expressions in Japanese. Successfully passing N5 indicates the ability to handle basic conversations and comprehend straightforward content in Japanese. This foundational proficiency sets the stage for further language development.
