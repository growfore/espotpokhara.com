<Section title="Why Study in USA from Nepal?">

The US higher education system is the most competitive and diverse in the world, with over 4,500 accredited colleges and universities offering Nepali students an extraordinary choice. Here is why we recommend the USA for the right student profile and why thousands of Nepali students choose it every year.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">Globally Recognized Degrees</h3>

A US degree is recognized by employers, governments, and universities worldwide without question. American institutions dominate the QS and Times Higher Education rankings, MIT, Harvard, Stanford, and Columbia consistently sit in the global top 25. When you graduate from a US university, that credential opens doors in Nepal, across Asia, and globally.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">Optional Practical Training (OPT), Up to 3 Years for STEM</h3>

F-1 students are eligible for Optional Practical Training (OPT), up to 12 months of post-graduation work authorization in the USA. If you graduate from a STEM program (Science, Technology, Engineering, or Mathematics), you qualify for an additional 24-month STEM OPT extension, giving you a total of 36 months to work in the USA without an employer-sponsored visa. This is one of the biggest advantages of a US education, and our counselors will help you identify STEM-designated programs that match your career goals.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">On-Campus Work Rights While Studying</h3>

F-1 students can work on-campus up to 20 hours per week during the semester and full-time during official vacation periods, no separate work permit needed. Off-campus work is available through CPT (Curricular Practical Training), which our team will guide you through when you are enrolled and eligible.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">Pathway to H-1B Visa and US Permanent Residency</h3>

While the USA does not offer a direct study-to-PR route like Canada, the pathway is well-established: F-1 → OPT → H-1B (employer-sponsored) → Employment-Based Green Card (EB-2 or EB-3). Many Nepali professionals have built long-term careers and permanent lives in the USA through this route. Importantly, Nepali nationals fall in the 'rest of world' green card queue, meaning significantly shorter wait times than Indian or Chinese applicants. Our team will brief you on the long-term immigration strategy from day one.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">Generous Scholarships and Funded Graduate Programs</h3>

US universities, particularly for graduate students, offer some of the most generous funding in the world. Teaching Assistantships (TAs) and Research Assistantships (RAs) at PhD and master's programs frequently include full tuition waivers plus monthly stipends. The Fulbright Scholarship, administered through the US Embassy in Kathmandu, is fully funded and available to Nepali students. We help our students identify and apply for the best funding options available for their profile.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">Safe, Diverse, and Academically Stimulating Environment</h3>

Over 1.1 million international students study in the USA, Nepal is consistently among the top sending countries. Established Nepali communities exist in major study cities, including New York, Boston, the Bay Area, and Washington DC. US campuses offer research opportunities, entrepreneurship ecosystems, and career development resources that are simply unmatched anywhere else in the world.

<Section title="Intakes in the USA for Nepali Students">

Timing your application correctly is critical. We guide our students through intake planning as part of our free initial counseling session.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">Fall Intake, August / September (Primary Intake)</h3>

Fall is the primary and most important intake across virtually all US universities. It offers the widest program availability, the most scholarship opportunities, and the largest incoming class. Applications for Fall 2026 typically close between November 2025 and January 2026, earlier for competitive programs. If you are targeting Fall 2026, contact us now to ensure your preparation timeline is on track.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">Spring Intake, January (Secondary Intake)</h3>

Spring intake is available at many universities with a smaller program selection, which is ideal for students who missed the Fall deadline or received a late decision. Applications typically close between August and October of the preceding year. Our team actively monitors Spring availability at partner universities and can match you to programs accepting Spring enrollment.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">Summer Intake, May / June (Limited Options)</h3>

Summer enrollment is mainly available for English language preparation programs, certificate courses, and select graduate programs. Some students use it as a strategic entry point before transitioning into a degree program in the fall. We will advise you on whether a summer entry pathway makes sense for your situation.

Not sure which intake suits your timeline? Talk to our USA counselors at Espot Pokhara Education & Visa Services, and we'll map out your application calendar for free.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">Financial Proof Requirements</h3>

The US Embassy requires you to demonstrate funds covering your entire program duration, not just the first year. This is stricter than most other countries. Your documents must clearly show:

- Full tuition for the program duration (as stated on your I-20 from the university)
- Living expenses for the full study period in the USA
- Return travel funds to Nepal
- Liquid evidence: bank statements, fixed deposit receipts, sponsor income proof, property documents

Our team reviews all financial documents before submission to ensure consistency, credibility, and completeness. A weak financial package is one of the top reasons F-1 visas are denied, we prevent that.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">Non-Immigrant Intent (Section 214(b)) Requirement</h3>

US immigration law presumes that visa applicants intend to immigrate. For an F-1 visa, you must overcome this presumption by demonstrating strong ties to Nepal, family, property, and clear career plans at home. The embassy officer assesses this at your interview.

This is why our mock interview preparation is so valuable: we coach you to answer 'Why the USA?', 'Why this university?', and 'What will you do after graduation?' with specific, honest, compelling answers that demonstrate your intent to return.

Most US universities automatically enroll international students in a university health insurance plan (typically USD 1,200–2,500/year, billed through your tuition account). The USA has no universal public healthcare, costs without insurance can be catastrophic. We brief all our students on health coverage requirements as part of pre-departure orientation.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">From OPT to H-1B, The Path Forward</h3>

<Section title="Long-Term USA Stay & Green Card Pathways for Nepali Students">

The USA does not have a direct study-to-PR route like Canada. However, the pathway from F-1 to permanent residency is well-established, and Nepali nationals have a key strategic advantage that our team explains to every student.

- Standard Route - F-1 to OPT to H-1B to Green Card: Graduate on F-1 → work on OPT (12–36 months) → H-1B lottery (employer-sponsored) → PERM Labor Certification → EB-2 or EB-3 employment-based green card. The H-1B lottery runs annually in March. US master's degree holders get 20,000 reserved visas above the regular 65,000 cap.
- EB-1A Extraordinary Ability: For Nepali graduates who achieve exceptional distinction in their field, researchers, artists, athletes, entrepreneurs, self-petition for a green card without employer sponsorship.
- EB-2 National Interest Waiver (NIW): For Nepali graduates with advanced degrees whose work serves the national interest of the USA, researchers, scientists, engineers, public health professionals. Self-petition without a job offer or PERM process.
- The Nepali Advantage: Unlike Indian or Chinese nationals who face EB-2/EB-3 green card backlogs of 20–50+ years, Nepali nationals fall in the 'rest of world' category, with typical EB-2/EB-3 wait times of 3–8 years. This is a real and significant advantage that our counsellors explain in full during our USA strategy sessions.

Want to understand your long-term immigration options in the USA? Our team provides a complete USA immigration strategy briefing as part of our counselling service, not after you've already paid fees elsewhere. Speak to us first.
