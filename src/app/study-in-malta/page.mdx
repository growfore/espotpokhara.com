<Section title="Intakes in Malta for Nepali Students">

Maltese institutions run a more flexible intake calendar than many destinations, which gives Nepali students multiple entry points across the year, but with Malta's longer visa processing time and the added step of applying without a local embassy, getting your timeline right matters even more here.

This is Malta's main academic year start and the intake with the widest course selection across universities and colleges. <h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">January/February Intake (Secondary Intake)</h3>

A solid mid-year intake available at many institutions, particularly useful for students who missed the September deadline or need additional time to complete financial documentation. Our team actively monitors which partner institutions are accepting applications for this intake.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">Rolling & Short-Course Intakes</h3>

Several Maltese colleges, particularly those offering foundation, diploma, and English-language preparation programs, accept applications on a rolling basis throughout the year. This can be a useful entry strategy for students who want to start sooner and transition into a full degree program afterward. We'll advise you on whether this pathway fits your academic goals.

<Section title="Malta Student Visa, Process & Requirements for Nepali Students">

Malta's visa process has one structural difference every Nepali applicant needs to understand upfront: Malta does not have an embassy in Nepal, and Nepal does not have an embassy in Malta. Applications are typically processed through the High Commission of Malta in New Delhi, India, often via VFS Global, though procedures and acceptable channels can shift, which is exactly why working with a consultancy that tracks the current process closely matters here more than for most destinations.

At Espot Pokhara Education & Visa Services, Malta visa preparation is one of our specializations precisely because of this added complexity. We review every document in your application file and guide you through each stage personally, including coordination with the New Delhi process where required.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">Academic Requirements</h3>

You'll need a confirmed enrollment or acceptance letter from an MFHEA-licensed institution in Malta. Requirements vary by program level and institution, our team reviews your academic transcripts before you apply to confirm exactly which programs you're eligible for.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">English Language Requirements</h3>

A notable advantage of Malta: IELTS is often not mandatory. Many institutions accept a Medium of Instruction (MOI) certificate confirming your prior education was conducted in English, which can simplify the admissions timeline considerably for Nepali students from English-medium schools and colleges. Where a test score is required, our team will advise on the specific requirement and arrange preparation accordingly.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">Financial Proof Requirements</h3>

You must demonstrate funds covering your tuition fees (paid in full or partially, depending on institution policy) plus approximately EUR 8,400–9,600 per year for living expenses, along with a clear, traceable source of funds. Acceptable evidence typically includes:

- 3–6 months of bank statements showing consistent, traceable funds
- A sponsor affidavit, where a family member or guardian is funding your studies
- Proof of accommodation in Malta for a minimum of 6 months

Our team reviews all financial documents before submission to ensure consistency and credibility, financial documentation is the single most common reason for Malta student visa rejections, and we work specifically to close that gap before you apply.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">Health Insurance Requirements</h3>

(Schengen-Compliant) Malta requires comprehensive health insurance with a minimum coverage of EUR 30,000, valid across the Schengen Area for the full duration of your intended stay. This must be arranged before your visa application and is non-negotiable for Schengen visa categories. We help you source compliant insurance as part of our visa support service.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">Statement of Purpose</h3>

A clear, honest, and specific Statement of Purpose explaining your choice of institution, program, and post-study plans strengthens your application meaningfully. Generic, copied SOPs are a known red flag in visa screening, our team reviews and refines every SOP before submission.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">Visa Document Checklist</h3>

Our team prepares and reviews every document in your Malta visa application file. The complete checklist includes:

- Valid Nepali passport (minimum 6 months' validity beyond intended stay, with at least 2 blank pages)
- Completed and signed visa application form
- ICAO/Schengen-standard passport-size photographs
- Official enrollment or acceptance letter from your Maltese institution
- Tuition fee payment proof (full or partial, per institution requirement)
- Financial evidence, bank statements and sponsor affidavit where applicable
- Schengen-compliant health insurance (minimum EUR 30,000 coverage)
- Proof of accommodation in Malta (minimum 6 months)
- Statement of Purpose
- Police clearance certificate from Nepal Police

<Section title="Part-Time Job Opportunities in Malta for Nepali Students">

Work rights in Malta are real but structured slightly differently than in other study destinations, understanding the timeline matters as much as understanding the rules themselves.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">The 90-Day Rule</h3>

International students must complete their first 90 days in Malta and hold a valid e-Residence Permit before they're permitted to begin part-time work. This isn't a formality to skip, working before this period or without the permit is a serious visa compliance issue, and we brief every student clearly on this before departure.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">During Studies</h3>

Once eligible, students can work up to 20 hours per week. Common roles for international students in Malta include hospitality, retail, tutoring, and roles within Malta's tourism and iGaming sectors, both of which have consistent demand for English-speaking staff.

<h3 className="font-heading text-heading-sm text-navy mt-6 mb-2">Job Search Process</h3>

Unlike some destinations where part-time work is largely unrestricted once authorized, Malta generally requires securing a job offer first, after which the employer applies for the relevant permit through JobsPlus, Malta's employment services authority. We guide students through this sequence so the process doesn't stall at the wrong step.

A student working 20 hours/week in Malta typically earns enough to meaningfully offset accommodation and food costs, particularly given Malta's already lower baseline cost of living compared to Western Europe.

</Section>
